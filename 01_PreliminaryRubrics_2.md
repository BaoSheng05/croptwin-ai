## Short Video Evaluation Criteria
| Element | Excellent (8-10) | Average (5-7) | Satisfactory (0-4) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Intro & Problem Statement** | Clearly introduces the theme with sufficient background and evidence. Well-defined solution idea supported by sources[cite: 10]. | Presents intro/problem with some background. Solution is clear but lacks depth[cite: 10]. | Unclear or lacks relevance to the theme[cite: 10]. |
| **Product Demonstration** | Clearly shows how the solution addresses the problem. Prototype handles essential functionalities smoothly[cite: 10]. | Prototype demonstrated with relevant features but lacks thorough explanation[cite: 10]. | Not effectively demonstrated or unrelated to identified problem[cite: 10]. |
| **Marketing** | Identifies target audience and market fit. Demonstrates commercial potential[cite: 10]. | Identifies audience but lacks depth or supporting data[cite: 10]. | Unclear or does not align with the solution[cite: 10]. |
| **Technology Architecture** | Well-structured architecture, scalable and flexible. Explanation is clear and detailed[cite: 10]. | Basic architecture with some explanation; scalability not well discussed[cite: 10]. | Unclear, incomplete, or unsuitable[cite: 10]. |
| **Video Duration** | Within 5 minutes[cite: 10]. | Exceeds limit by less than one minute[cite: 10]. | Significantly shorter or longer than the specified range[cite: 10]. |

## Prototype Evaluation Criteria
| Element | Excellent (8-10) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Overall Appearance** | Well-built, visually appealing, and demonstrates strong craftsmanship[cite: 10]. |
| **Creativity/Innovation** | Introduces a novel idea or significant improvement to existing solutions[cite: 10]. |
| **Functionality** | Fully meets requirements, is intuitive, and integrates components seamlessly[cite: 10]. |
| **Impact** | High potential for real-world impact, scalability, and sustainability[cite: 10]. |
| **Relevance** | Effectively addresses a meaningful problem with strong real-world applicability[cite: 10]. |
